What is Mutual Aid?

Mutual aid is people supporting each other directly — no middlemen, no bureaucracy. We pool small resources to meet shared needs quickly and transparently. It’s been around for centuries and shows up today as community bail funds, food banks, community fridges, repair circles, and co-ops. Historically, Black mutual aid societies, labor union benefit funds, and friendly societies did this long before modern safety nets existed. Learn more on Wikipedia.

A community fridge — a modern example of mutual aid, where neighbors share free food for anyone in need.

Examples: Community bail funds, food banks & fridges, neighborhood tool/repair circles, co-ops, historical lodge & friendly societies, union emergency funds.

Van Breakdown Fund

A dedicated emergency pot for major repairs — transmissions, engines, tires — to keep members moving. Requests are reviewed by the group; if approved, the funds go out fast so no one is stranded.

Example: A member blows a transmission in a remote area. The group approves $1,000 from the fund and the repair gets handled within days.

Land Fund

A pooled investment to buy land together — for shared camping, events, food production, and long-term security. Members contribute regularly; the land becomes a shared asset managed by the group under clear rules.

Example: Over five years, contributions and appreciation enable the group to buy a multi-acre property that hosts gatherings, offers seasonal camping, and serves as a safe base.

What Is This?

We’re building a mutual aid network for vanlifers: a shared emergency fund to help each other survive and thrive on the road. Small monthly contributions from each member grow over time, and can be used when someone in the group needs support.

Example: 10 members contribute $10/month (about 20,000 sats). That builds a $1,200 yearly fund to help cover breakdowns, urgent travel, or health costs.

Why Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is decentralized digital money — no banks, no middlemen, and it can’t be inflated away. It’s ideal for nomads: portable, secure, and accessible anywhere. We store the fund in Bitcoin so it can grow and remain sovereign.

Example: If the Bitcoin price rises, our shared fund grows too — without needing extra contributions. 1,000,000 sats saved at $30k/Bitcoin is $300. If BTC hits $60k, it's $600.

How Multisig Works

The fund is held in a multisig wallet: a shared vault that needs multiple people to approve a withdrawal. Think of it like a safe that takes 3 out of 5 keys to open. No one can spend the funds alone — decisions are made together.

Example: 5 trusted members hold keys. Any 3 must agree to release funds. This ensures security without central control.

How Claims Work

If someone needs help, they submit a simple request explaining their situation. The group discusses and, if enough signers approve (e.g. 3 out of 5), the funds are sent. It’s fast, fair, and based on community trust.

Example: Alex’s van breaks down in the desert. They message the group, share a repair quote, and 3 signers approve $400 in aid. Funds are released the same day.

Transparency & Accountability

Every transaction can be publicly verified on the Bitcoin blockchain — anyone in the group can see when and where funds move. We also keep a simple shared log of all decisions, proposals, and votes. It’s all open and auditable by the community.

How Mutual Aid Grows Over Time

As members contribute monthly and the Bitcoin price increases, the fund gains value even without more members. This allows the group to support larger or more frequent requests over time.

Example: In Year 1, the group helps one member with $500. By Year 3, the same monthly contributions plus BTC appreciation allow for three separate $500 claims per year without draining the fund.
