What is Mutual Aid?

Mutual aid is when communities voluntarily help each other meet needs, without hierarchy or profit. It’s about solidarity, not charity. Historically, mutual aid societies were the backbone of community support before government programs like Social Security or Medicare existed.

Learn more from the Mutual Aid Wikipedia page.

A community fridge — a modern example of mutual aid, where neighbors share free food for anyone in need.

Van Breakdown Fund

This fund helps members cover urgent repairs to keep them safely on the road. Whether it’s a transmission failure, engine trouble, or replacing a critical part, the Van Breakdown Fund ensures no one is stranded without help.

Example: Jamie’s alternator fails in the middle of a road trip. The fund covers the $450 repair, and Jamie is back on the road the next day.

Land Fund

The Land Fund is for pooling resources to acquire land for shared use — a home base for members, a safe place to park, host gatherings, and build community projects. Contributions grow over time and are used toward purchase, maintenance, and improvements.

Example: The group pools funds for 3 years and buys a few acres near a national forest. Members can camp there for free and host seasonal meetups.
